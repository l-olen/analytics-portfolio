## Forecast Methodology

### Base Scenario
- Monthly forecast built on 2025 actuals preserving seasonality
- Funnel conversion rates held constant at 2025 levels
- Deal value per signed deal held at scenario-specific avg (reflects expected product mix shift)

### Adjustment 1: Product 1 Paid Channels (global budget increase)
"Strongly promoted" interpreted as significant budget increase; exact amount not specified.
Three scenarios modelled using diminishing returns logic (lead growth < budget growth at scale):

| Scenario | Budget increase | Lead increase | Elasticity |
|----------|----------------|---------------|------------|
| Conservative | +20% | +15% | 0.75 |
| Base | +40% | +28% | 0.70 |
| Aggressive | +60% | +38% | 0.63 |

- Organic Product 1 traffic unchanged (paid-only promotion)
- Rationale: auction-based platforms show diminishing returns at scale; audience saturation risk
- Limitation: conversion rate quality declines at scale -- modelled as degradation (see table below)

### Adjustment 2: LatAm +20% All Channels
- Conservative: +20% spend вЖТ +12% leads
- Base: +20% spend вЖТ +15% leads
- Aggressive: +20% spend вЖТ +20% leads (best-channel concentration assumed)
- Limitation: elasticity coefficient is judgment-based; actual response depends on market saturation

### Adjustment 3: German Locale Deletion
Germany represents ~35% of European leads, ~33% of European signed deals, ~38% of European deal value. German avg deal (~30,000 EUR) exceeds European avg (26,693 EUR) -- high-value market.

Impact by channel type:

| Channel type | Conservative | Base | Aggressive |
|-------------|-------------|------|------------|
| Organic (google/bing) | -50% German traffic | -60% | -70% |
| Direct / (none) | -20% | -30% | -40% |
| Paid channels | -5% | -10% | -15% |

Rationale: organic loses localized SEO rankings; direct users partially adapt; paid ads continue in English with reduced conversion for German-speaking users.

Additional risks not fully quantified:
- SEO backlink equity and hreflang structure may cause broader organic decline beyond German pages
- Austria, Switzerland, and German-speaking operators in other EU markets (~5-10% additional European leads)

## Interaction Effect: Product 1 x LatAm
Product 1 is sold globally including LatAm. Both adjustments applied multiplicatively:

**Key forecast insight:** Base and Aggressive scenarios produce the same signed deal count (122), because conversion rate degradation in Aggressive offsets the higher lead volume. Deal value is still higher in Aggressive (+2.1% vs Base) due to the avg deal value assumption reflecting greater Product 1 mix. This confirms that aggressive paid scaling improves revenue through mix shift, not volume of deals.

**Recommended interpretation:**
- **Base** = operational planning case
- **Conservative** = downside bound (Germany impact stronger than expected, or P1 elasticity lower)
- **Aggressive** = upside bound (LatAm responds well, P1 demand not yet saturated)
- Monitor Europe separately -- locale changes may create non-linear demand impact

## Key Assumptions and Limitations
1. Diminishing returns elasticity coefficients are judgment-based -- no historical spend-response data available to validate
2. Deal value per signed deal held at scenario-specific avg by scenario -- assumes no pricing changes
3. Macro, competitive, regulatory, and sales capacity factors not modelled
4. German locale impact may be underestimated if SEO backlink/hreflang effects are significant
5. Austria/Switzerland spillover effect not quantified due to data unavailability
6. Seasonality based on single year (2025) -- no multi-year history available for more robust model
7. Product 4 high avg deal (31,973 EUR) not explicitly modelled in forecast -- if P4 qualification improves, upside exists
